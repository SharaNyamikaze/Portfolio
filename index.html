Analysis of various blood samples found in a crime scene for species origin test and blood grouping to find out the suspects. The analysis of the provided samples were done by both Presumptive(Colour tests) and Confirmatory tests(Crystal tests). In forensic use, a presumptive test indicating the possible presence of blood is an invaluable tool. This type of test has particular value in screening out samples that are definitely not blood and do not require further testing. Used in this manner, a test should be sensitive to some component of blood which remains even after the blood has dried, aged, or become diluted. This ideal test should also be specific. These tests are those which ‘confirms’ the prescence of blood positively. To find out the presence of blood, crystal tests are regarded as the confirmatory tests. It has smaller risk of false positive tests but is more costly.
